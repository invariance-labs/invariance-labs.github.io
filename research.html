Minority-Class Failure and Confidence Miscalibration in Electrocardiogram Classification: A Cross-Dataset Error Audit

We audited seven ML classifiers across three independent ECG datasets and found that classification errors are not randomly distributed — they concentrate in minority classes and specific patients, and a model's confidence is an unreliable, model-family-dependent signal of correctness.
